# How to Build Cold Audiences for Meta Ads: The Interest Stacking Strategy That Finds Your Best Customers

**Meta Description:** Build cold audiences for Meta Ads that actually convert using interest stacking. 3 audience blueprints with specific targeting examples for coaches and consultants.
**Target Keywords:** cold audiences meta ads, interest stacking facebook ads
**Estimated Read Time:** 10 min

---

**TL;DR:** Cold audiences are people who have never heard of you. Interest stacking combines 3--5 related interests into a single ad set to create an audience of 1--5 million people that Meta's algorithm can optimize. This guide gives you three proven audience blueprints for coaching and consulting businesses, plus the rules for testing and scaling.

---

Your lead magnet funnel is built. Your tracking is set up. Your email sequence is ready. Now you need strangers to see it.

Cold audiences are how you reach people who have never visited your website, joined your list, or heard your name. They are the top of your growth funnel. Get this right, and you have a predictable, scalable source of leads. Get it wrong, and you burn through your ad budget showing ads to people who will never convert.

The biggest mistake I see with cold targeting is going too narrow ("AI consultants in Austin who also like Elon Musk") or too broad ("all entrepreneurs in the US"). Interest stacking finds the sweet spot.

---

## What Is Interest Stacking?

Interest stacking means combining multiple related interests into a single ad set. Instead of targeting one narrow interest, you layer 3--5 interests together using Meta's **Detailed Targeting** OR logic.

When you add multiple interests in the same targeting field, Meta shows your ad to anyone who matches **any** of the selected interests. This expands your audience while keeping it relevant.

**Example:**
- Interest 1: "Entrepreneurship" (200M people)
- Interest 2: "Small business owners" (50M people)
- Interest 3: "Digital marketing" (150M people)
- Interest 4: "Sales funnel" (5M people)
- Interest 5: "Business coaching" (30M people)

Combined with US targeting and age 25--55, this creates an audience of roughly 2--4 million people. Large enough for the algorithm to optimize, focused enough to be relevant.

## How to Set Up Interest Stacking in Ads Manager

1. Create a new campaign with the **Leads** objective ([see our campaign setup guide](/tutorials/how-to-set-up-your-first-meta-ad-campaign-for-lea))
2. At the **Ad Set** level, go to **Audience**
3. Set Location, Age, Gender
4. Under **Detailed Targeting**, click **Browse** or **Search**
5. Search for your first interest and select it
6. Click **Suggestions** to find related interests
7. Add all 3--5 interests in the same field (they combine with OR logic)
8. Check the **Estimated Audience Size** on the right -- target 1--5 million
9. Leave **Placements** on Advantage+ (automatic)
10. Save the audience for reuse

**Critical rule:** Do NOT use the **Narrow Audience** button. Narrowing requires users to match ALL interests (AND logic), which shrinks your audience too much for the algorithm to optimize.

## Testing Framework: The 3-Audience Test

Run all three audiences simultaneously with the same ad creative. This tells you which audience responds best to your message.

**Test setup:**
- 1 Campaign (Leads objective)
- 3 Ad Sets (one per audience stack)
- Same ad creative in all 3 ad sets
- $10--15/day per ad set ($30--45/day total)
- Run for 7 days minimum

**After 7 days, evaluate:**

| Metric | Good | Action |
|--------|------|--------|
| CTR > 1% | Audience is engaged | Keep running |
| CTR < 0.5% | Audience is not resonating | Pause or test new creative |
| CPL < $10 | Viable audience | Scale budget by 20% |
| CPL > $15 | Too expensive | Test different interest combinations |
| Lead quality | Leads opening emails, clicking links | This is the winner |

**The winner** is not always the cheapest CPL. It is the audience that produces leads who open your emails, click your links, and book calls. A $7 lead who books a call is worth more than a $3 lead who never opens an email.

## Exclusions: Who NOT to Show Ads To

Always exclude these audiences to avoid wasting budget:

1. **Website visitors** (Custom Audience) -- they are not cold anymore; retarget them separately
2. **Email list** (Customer List) -- they are already leads; nurture them via email
3. **People who have purchased** -- do not spend cold traffic budget on existing customers
4. **Page followers** -- they can see your organic content for free

**How to add exclusions:**
1. In the Ad Set, scroll to **Custom Audiences**
2. Click **Exclude**
3. Select or create the audiences above

## Scaling What Works

Once you have a winning audience (consistent CPL below your target for 7+ days):

### Horizontal Scaling (Lower Risk)
- Duplicate the winning ad set
- Change 1--2 interests to test variations
- Run alongside the original
- Example: Replace "Business coaching" with "Online courses" in the Entrepreneur Stack

### Vertical Scaling (Higher Risk)
- Increase the winning ad set's budget by **20% every 3 days**
- Never increase by more than 20% at once (resets the learning phase)
- Monitor CPL daily after each increase
- If CPL spikes, pause for 48 hours and resume at the previous budget

### Lookalike Audiences (After 100+ Conversions)
Once your pixel has 100+ Lead events, create a Lookalike Audience:
1. Go to Audiences > Create Audience > Lookalike Audience
2. Source: Your Meta Pixel "Lead" event
3. Location: United States
4. Size: 1% (most similar to your converters)

Lookalikes typically outperform interest-based audiences because they are modeled on people who actually converted, not just people with matching interests.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is a cold audience in Facebook ads?
A cold audience consists of people who have never interacted with your brand -- no website visits, no email list, no social media engagement. You reach them through interest-based targeting, broad targeting, or lookalike audiences.

### How many interests should I stack in one ad set?
3--5 interests per ad set. Fewer than 3 creates too narrow an audience for the algorithm. More than 5 dilutes the focus and makes it harder to identify which interests drive results.

### What audience size should I target?
For cold prospecting, target 1--5 million people. Below 500K, the algorithm lacks data to optimize. Above 10M, you are paying for too many irrelevant impressions. In between gives Meta enough room to find your ideal customers.

### Should I use Advantage+ Audience instead of manual targeting?
For your first campaigns, use manual interest stacking so you learn which audiences work. Once you have strong pixel data (100+ conversions), test Advantage+ Audience -- it uses Meta's AI to find converters, sometimes outperforming manual targeting.

### How long should I run a cold audience test?
Minimum 7 days. The algorithm needs 3--5 days to exit the learning phase, and you need 2+ days of post-learning data to evaluate real performance. Do not judge or change anything before day 5.

## Key Takeaways

- Stack **3--5 related interests** per ad set using OR logic (not AND)
- Target audience size of **1--5 million** for cold prospecting
- Test **3 audience stacks** simultaneously with identical creative
- **Exclude** website visitors, email list, and customers from cold campaigns
- Scale winners by **20% every 3 days** (vertical) or test interest variations (horizontal)
- Build **Lookalike Audiences** after 100+ pixel conversions
